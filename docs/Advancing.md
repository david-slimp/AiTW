# Advancing (Discipleship Actions + Verses)

Purpose: build a large, curated list of Bible verses that define what a faithful disciple does, to guide in-game goals, prompts, and hinting.

## How this file will be used
- Provide in-game guidance (Bible reading, Holy Spirit prompts).
- Define behavior goals (service, repentance, evangelism, fruit, stewardship).
- Inform command design (e.g., REPENT, READ, SERVE, GIVE, ENCOURAGE).

## Game Implications (Verbs + Systems)
- REPENT: resets guilt/conviction state; restores clarity after missteps.
- READ: unlocks guidance prompts and reveals Scripture-based hints.
- PRAY: opens insight prompts, reduces fear, and stabilizes the player.
- SERVE: advances service arcs and opens relational gates.
- ENCOURAGE: improves NPC trust and unlocks dialogue paths.
- GIVE: advances generosity/mercy arcs; impacts stewardship score.
- CONFESS: clears burdens and removes distorted narration.
- WITNESS: advances evangelism arcs and unlocks discipleship events.

System mapping (high-level):
- Fruit of the Spirit -> capability unlocks (dialogue options, puzzle paths).
- Conviction/peace -> guidance system that nudges the player.
- Fellowship -> group access to resources, hints, and co-op gates.

## Summary of New Testament Teaching (Foundation)
The New Testament frames Christian actions as evidence of a true relationship with Christ, not a means of earning salvation. These are presented as moral commands, spiritual disciplines, and the fruit of a transformed life.

### 1) The Core Commands of Jesus
- Love God: Love the Lord your God with all your heart, soul, and mind.
- Love Your Neighbor: Love others as yourself, including enemies.
- The Golden Rule: Do unto others as you would have them do unto you.
- The New Commandment: “Love one another as I have loved you.” (John 13:34)

### 2) Character and Internal State (The “Be” List)
- Beatitudes: Be humble (poor in spirit), merciful, pure in heart, and peacemakers.
- Fruit of the Spirit: Cultivate love, joy, peace, patience, kindness, goodness, faithfulness, gentleness, self-control.
- Be Transformed: Do not be conformed to the world; renew your mind to discern God’s will.
- Be Holy: Strive for holiness in all conduct, imitating God’s character.
- Be Forgiving: Forgive others exactly as God has forgiven you.

### 3) Spiritual Disciplines (Connecting to God)
- Prayer: Pray continually and with transparency.
- Study of Scripture: Regularly read, study, and memorize God’s Word.
- Fasting: Practice self-denial for spiritual focus, typically in secret.
- Worship: Offer wholehearted praise to God, privately and in community.
- Fellowship: Participate in the local church for encouragement and accountability.

### 4) Daily Actions and Social Responsibilities
- Generosity: Give to the poor and share resources without expecting return.
- Service: Use time and talents to serve others, especially the “least of these.”
- Honesty: Speak truth; let your “yes” be yes and your “no” be no.
- Great Commission: Share the Gospel and make disciples of all nations.
- Non-Retaliation: Do not repay evil for evil; turn the other cheek and bless persecutors.
- Stewardship: Treat your body as the temple of the Holy Spirit; manage finances as a steward.

### 5) Things to Avoid (“Put Off”)
- Moral Sins: Sexual immorality, impurity, lust, greed.
- Social Sins: Anger, rage, malice, slander, lying, filthy language.
- Internal Sins: Pride, hypocrisy, covetousness.

## Structured Map of New Testament Discipleship (Core -> Daily -> Relational -> Mission)
This section expands the foundation with a more complete, ordered map. It distinguishes required foundations, expected obedience, and fruit that grows through abiding in Christ.

### 1) Core Foundations (Required, Not Optional)
These are essential, not suggestions.

#### A) Believe in and Trust Jesus Christ
- Believe He is Lord, Savior, Son of God.
- Trust His death and resurrection for salvation.
- References: John 3:16, Romans 10:9–10, John 14:6.
- Christianity begins with who you trust, not what you do.

#### B) Repent (Turn Away) from Sin
- Ongoing posture, not a one-time event.
- Includes confession and turning toward obedience.
- References: Luke 13:3, Acts 3:19, 1 John 1:7–9.

#### C) Be Born Again (Regeneration by the Spirit)
- New heart, new nature, new desires.
- References: John 3:3–8, Titus 3:5.

#### D) Submit to Jesus as Lord
- Not merely Savior; willing obedience.
- References: Luke 6:46, Matthew 7:21.
- “Why do you call me ‘Lord’ and not do what I say?”

### 2) Moral & Ethical Obedience (Expected Evidence)
Not salvation by works, but salvation produces obedience.

#### A) Turn Away from Known, Willful Sin
Repeated across many passages:
- Sexual immorality, adultery, theft, murder, lying, greed.
- Drunkenness, idolatry.
- References: 1 Corinthians 6:9–11, Galatians 5:19–21, Ephesians 5:3–7.
- Persistent, unrepentant sin is treated as evidence of not walking with Christ.

#### B) Obey Christ’s Teachings
Not just the Ten Commandments, but Jesus’ heart-level teaching:
- No hatred (murder of the heart).
- No lust (adultery of the heart).
- Love enemies.
- Forgive others.
- Practice honesty.
- References: Matthew 5–7, John 14:15.

### 3) Inner Transformation (Fruit, Not Force)
These are not “try harder” rules; they grow in a Spirit-led life.

#### A) Produce the Fruit of the Spirit
- Love, joy, peace, patience, kindness, goodness, faithfulness, gentleness, self-control.
- Reference: Galatians 5:22–23.
- Fruit grows from abiding, not striving.

#### B) Renew the Mind
- Let Scripture reshape thinking.
- Reject worldly values.
- References: Romans 12:1–2, Colossians 3:1–10.

#### C) Walk by the Holy Spirit
- Dependence, sensitivity, obedience.
- References: Galatians 5:16, Romans 8:1–14.

### 4) Relationship with God (Daily Practices)
Strongly emphasized without checklist legalism.

#### A) Prayer
- Regular, sincere communication.
- Includes thanksgiving, confession, intercession.
- References: Matthew 6:5–13, 1 Thessalonians 5:17.

#### B) Scripture Intake
- Reading, meditating, obeying.
- References: Matthew 4:4, 2 Timothy 3:16–17.

#### C) Worship
- Corporate and personal.
- In spirit and truth.
- References: John 4:23–24, Romans 12:1.

### 5) Love in Action (Non-Negotiable Evidence)
Jesus treats this as the defining marker of discipleship.

#### A) Love God
- Reference: Matthew 22:37.

#### B) Love Others
- Fellow believers, neighbors, enemies.
- References: John 13:34–35, Matthew 22:39, Romans 12.
- “By this all people will know that you are my disciples…”

#### C) Forgive Others
- Repeated emphasis; tied to spiritual health.
- References: Matthew 6:14–15, Matthew 18:21–35.

#### D) Serve Others Humbly
- References: Mark 10:43–45, Philippians 2:3–8.

### 6) Community & Church Life (Strong Expectation)
Not optional add-ons.

#### A) Fellowship with Other Believers
- References: Hebrews 10:24–25, Acts 2:42.

#### B) Use Spiritual Gifts to Build Others
- References: 1 Corinthians 12, Romans 12:4–8.

#### C) Submit to Godly Leadership (Not Abuse)
- Reference: Hebrews 13:17.

### 7) Mission & Witness (Normal Christian Life)
Not just for pastors or missionaries.

#### A) Make Disciples
- Reference: Matthew 28:18–20.

#### B) Share the Gospel
- References: Romans 1:16, 1 Peter 3:15.

#### C) Live as Salt and Light
- Reference: Matthew 5:13–16.

### 8) Money, Possessions & Stewardship
Frequently addressed.

#### A) Generosity
- References: 2 Corinthians 9:6–8, Luke 12:15.

#### B) Caring for the Poor
- References: James 1:27, Matthew 25:31–46.

### 9) Perseverance & Growth

#### A) Endure Trials Faithfully
- References: James 1:2–4, Romans 5:3–5.

#### B) Continue in Faith
- References: Colossians 1:21–23, Hebrews 12:1–2.

### 10) What the NT Does Not Teach
- No sinless perfection in this life.
- No earning salvation by good works.
- No cultural Christianity without transformation.
- No private faith with no fruit.
- References: Ephesians 2:8–10, James 2:17.

### One-Sentence Summary
The New Testament teaches that true Christians are saved by grace through faith in Christ, and that this faith produces repentance, obedience, love, spiritual fruit, community life, and a growing desire to live like Jesus.
